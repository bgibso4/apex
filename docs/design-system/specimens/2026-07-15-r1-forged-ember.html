Direction A — Round 1

Forged Ember

Warmth and effort. The world shifts from blue-black to forge-black — warm near-black backgrounds with ember orange as the single hero accent. It keeps today's instrument-panel readouts, trading the cool software feel for something physical, like heated metal. The gym is warm; the app should be too.

Card language

  • Cards are matte and flat — no drop shadows, no gradients; 1px warm-hairline borders carry all sense of elevation.
  • Ember accent appears only on the current action and the primary CTA — never as decoration.
  • Insets (card-inset, surface) run one step warmer than their parent, not lighter — depth reads as heat, not brightness.

Liquid Glass stance & motion

Blend. Liquid Glass renders the chrome — tab bar and sheets — with a warm tint that matches the ember palette. Content cards stay matte and fully opaque, so the glass never competes with the data being read mid-set.

Tensions to explore

  • Warn-amber vs. ember proximity: the warning state moves to gold to keep clear of the accent — is that enough separation?
  • Does warm-black read as "premium" or as "brown" on OLED at low brightness?
  • The HYP block color is set to the accent — the same overload the indigo carried today. Keep it, or break the tie?
  • SF Pro Rounded for hero numerals — warmer, or too soft?
