Believes: sumi ink on aged paper, inverted for night. The field is warm ink-black (the halo reads as an ink bleed, not a lamp), text is paper-warm white, and the accent is an aged vermillion-clay — the color of a hanko seal: rare, small, and always meaningful. The Japanese-ness is carried by restraint you feel more than see: a touch more negative space around titles and between cards (ma — the pause as material), never by motif or ornament. Moss keeps its "earned" job and sits naturally in the warm field. Watch for: vermillion-clay vs moss both being earthy — they must never appear at equal visual weight, or the two-hue clarity blurs.

Believes: fog and iron — rune-stone minimalism. The field is fog-gray (barely cooler than neutral, far grayer than the rejected glacial Summit), and the accent is weathered steel: the grayest accent of any candidate, like iron that's seen weather. Nothing shines in the north; strength is assumed, not announced. The viking-ness is temperament — austere surfaces, blunt hierarchy, zero ornament — not runes or motifs. Moss reads as lichen on stone here, fitting the palette naturally. Watch for: the steel accent being too quiet to carry "now" at arm's length mid-set — the outline-vs-fill states have to work harder than with the blue.
